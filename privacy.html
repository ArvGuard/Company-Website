Who We Are

ArvGuard, Inc. ("ArvGuard," "we," "our," or "us") is a digital executor platform operated as a corporation incorporated in Delaware. We provide a secure vault service that allows individuals to store critical digital account information and designate trusted executors who can access that information upon verified incapacitation or death.

This Privacy Policy applies to all users of arvguard.com, arvguard.app, and any related mobile applications or services (collectively, the "Service").

Questions? Contact us at privacy@arvguard.com.

02

Information We Collect

We collect only what is necessary to operate the Service safely and effectively. We categorize data into two types: information we can read, and information we mathematically cannot read.

Information We Can Access
  • Account information: Name, email address, account creation date
  • Phone number (optional): If provided for SMS alive-check reminders or account verification. Opt-in is required and explicit. Retained with opt-in timestamp for TCPA compliance
  • Executor contacts: Names and email addresses of your designated executors (not encrypted, as we must contact them)
  • Alive check configuration: Your check-in schedule, escalation preferences, and current alive check state
  • Usage data: Login timestamps, IP addresses, device type, browser/OS for security and fraud prevention
  • Communications: Support emails, feedback, and other messages you send us
  • Payment data: Billing information processed through Stripe, Inc. We do not store full card numbers — Stripe handles all payment data under their PCI-compliant systems
🔒 Information We Cannot Access (Zero-Knowledge)
  • Vault contents: All documents, passwords, account credentials, and files stored in your vault are encrypted on your device before transmission. We receive only ciphertext — the decryption key never leaves your device
  • Vault item metadata: Names, labels, and categories you assign to vault items are also encrypted client-side
  • Secret shares: Your Shamir's Secret Sharing key fragments are never combined or accessible on our servers

This is not a policy choice — it is a mathematical guarantee. AES-256-GCM encryption with client-side key derivation (Argon2id) means your vault content is inaccessible to us by design.

We do not collect Social Security numbers, government ID numbers, biometric data, or health information.

03

Zero-Knowledge Architecture

ArvGuard, Inc. is built on a zero-knowledge architecture. This means your vault is encrypted on your device using a key derived from your master password before any data is transmitted to our servers. We store only encrypted ciphertext — the plaintext content of your vault is never accessible to ArvGuard, Inc. employees, systems, or legal process.

How It Works
  • Your master password is processed through Argon2id to derive a Vault Master Key (VMK)
  • Individual vault items are encrypted with unique Data Encryption Keys (DEKs) using AES-256-GCM
  • DEKs are wrapped (encrypted) with your VMK before storage
  • Your VMK never leaves your device in plaintext form
  • Executor access uses Shamir's Secret Sharing (2-of-3) — no single party holds a complete key

Important consequence: If you forget your master password, we cannot recover your vault contents. There is no "forgot password" that unlocks your encrypted data. We can only reset your account (deleting your vault) or help you initiate your recovery process if you have set one up.

Sharing & Disclosure

We do not sell your personal information. We share data only in these limited circumstances:

  • Service providers: Trusted vendors who help us operate the Service, including AWS (cloud infrastructure and storage), Stripe (payment processing), SendGrid (email delivery via AWS End User Messaging), and Twilio (SMS — toll-free number +18555607767). These providers are contractually bound to use your data only to provide services to ArvGuard, Inc.
  • Executor release: When a verified release process completes, we facilitate the delivery of encrypted vault contents to your designated executor(s). They receive the same ciphertext we store — decryption requires the Shamir key reconstruction process and cannot be performed by ArvGuard, Inc.
  • Legal requirements: We may disclose information if required by valid legal process (court order, subpoena). Because we cannot decrypt vault contents, any such disclosure would include only unencrypted account metadata described in Section 2
  • Business transfer: In the event of a merger, acquisition, or sale of assets, your information may be transferred. We will notify you and honor existing privacy commitments
  • Safety: We may disclose information to protect the safety of any person or to prevent fraud or illegal activity
06

Data Retention

We retain your personal information for as long as your account is active or as needed to provide services. Specific retention periods:

  • Account data: Retained while your account is active and for 2 years after deletion, then permanently purged
  • Encrypted vault contents: Retained until you delete them or close your account. Vault deletion is immediate and irreversible
  • Audit logs: Retained for 7 years (required for executor process integrity and legal compliance)
  • Payment records: Retained for 7 years (tax and financial compliance)
  • Support communications: Retained for 3 years after resolution
  • SMS opt-in records: Retained for the life of the account plus 4 years (TCPA compliance)

You may request deletion of your account at any time. Upon deletion, we will purge your personal data on the schedule above, except where retention is required by law. Account deletion cascades through all systems including Stripe customer records and Twilio phone records.

07

Security

ArvGuard, Inc. uses industry-standard and beyond-standard security practices:

  • AES-256-GCM encryption for all vault contents (client-side, before transmission)
  • Argon2id key derivation (resistant to GPU and ASIC brute-force attacks)
  • TLS 1.3 for all data in transit
  • Multi-factor authentication (email default, TOTP optional upgrade)
  • Immutable audit logging with 50+ event types
  • AWS infrastructure with SOC 2-compliant controls
  • Opaque refresh tokens to prevent token enumeration
  • Cloudflare WAF with bot protection and DDoS mitigation
  • Regular security reviews and penetration testing

No system is 100% secure. We encourage you to use a strong, unique master password and enable MFA on your account. In the event of a security breach affecting your unencrypted account data, we will notify you as required by law within 72 hours of becoming aware of the breach.

California Privacy Rights (CCPA / CPRA)

If you are a California resident, the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA) as amended by the California Privacy Rights Act (CPRA) grants you the following rights:

  • Right to Know: Request disclosure of the categories and specific pieces of personal information collected, the purposes for collection, and the categories of third parties with whom it is shared
  • Right to Delete: Request deletion of personal information we have collected, subject to certain legal exceptions (such as audit log retention)
  • Right to Correct: Request correction of inaccurate personal information we hold about you
  • Right to Opt-Out of Sale/Sharing: We do not sell or share personal information as defined by the CCPA. No opt-out is necessary, but you may request written confirmation at privacy@arvguard.com
  • Right to Limit Use of Sensitive Personal Information: We treat all personal data as sensitive and do not use it beyond what is necessary to provide the Service
  • Right to Non-Discrimination: We will not deny you services, charge different prices, or provide a different quality of service for exercising any CCPA right
Categories of Personal Information Collected (CCPA Classification)
  • Identifiers: Name, email address, IP address, device identifiers
  • Commercial information: Subscription plan, payment history (no card numbers)
  • Internet or network activity: Login timestamps, device type, browser/OS
  • Geolocation: Approximate only, derived from IP address — no precise location
  • Phone number: Only if voluntarily provided for SMS opt-in

We do not collect: biometric identifiers, health/medical information, racial or ethnic origin, political opinions, or precise geolocation.

Sensitive Personal Information (CCPA 2026)

Under CCPA 2026, personal information of individuals under 16 is classified as sensitive. ArvGuard, Inc. does not knowingly collect information from anyone under 18. Vault contents are zero-knowledge encrypted and are not accessible to ArvGuard, Inc. regardless of classification.

To submit a CCPA request, email privacy@arvguard.com with "CCPA Request" in the subject line. We will respond within 45 days. You may designate an authorized agent to submit requests on your behalf with written authorization.

EU and UK Users — GDPR Rights

If you are located in the European Economic Area (EEA) or United Kingdom, the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) or UK GDPR grants you specific rights regarding your personal data.

  • Lawful Basis for Processing: Contract performance (providing the Service you signed up for) and legitimate interests (security, fraud prevention, service integrity)
  • Right of Access (Article 15): Obtain confirmation of whether we process your data and receive a copy of it
  • Right to Rectification (Article 16): Request correction of inaccurate or incomplete personal data
  • Right to Erasure / Right to be Forgotten (Article 17): Request deletion of your personal data where there is no overriding legitimate reason to continue processing
  • Right to Restrict Processing (Article 18): Request that we limit processing of your personal data in certain circumstances
  • Right to Data Portability (Article 20): Receive your personal data in a structured, commonly-used, machine-readable format
  • Right to Object (Article 21): Object to processing based on legitimate interests or for direct marketing (which we do not engage in)
  • Rights Related to Automated Decision-Making (Article 22): ArvGuard's alive check system uses automated scheduling. You may request human review of any automated outcome by contacting support@arvguard.com
International Data Transfers

ArvGuard, Inc. is incorporated in Delaware with operations in the United States. If you are in the EEA or UK, your personal data is transferred to and processed in the United States. We implement appropriate safeguards including Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs) with our service providers where required. Security Breach Notification

In the event of a security breach affecting your unencrypted personal information (such as your email address, name, alive check configuration, or contact records), ArvGuard, Inc. will:

  • Notify affected users by email within 72 hours of becoming aware of the breach, or as required by applicable law
  • Notify relevant regulatory authorities as required by law (including GDPR supervisory authorities within 72 hours of discovery)
  • Describe in plain language what data was affected, how the breach occurred, and what steps we are taking to address and prevent recurrence
  • Provide specific guidance on steps you can take to protect yourself
  • Provide a dedicated contact for questions about the incident

Because vault contents are zero-knowledge encrypted with AES-256-GCM, a breach of ArvGuard's servers would not expose your vault data. Attackers would obtain only ciphertext that cannot be decrypted without your master password — a key that never leaves your device.

To report a security vulnerability, contact security@arvguard.com. We take all security reports seriously, investigate promptly, and respond to good-faith disclosures within 48 hours.
